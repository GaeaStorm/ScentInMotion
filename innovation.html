01

Fragrance Selection: How this differs from Layering

Luxury fragrance is built on olfactive architecture, not arbitrary blending. With Scent in Motion, each fragrance begins with a complete couture base composition designed by the perfumer as a finished scent. The modulation capsules are then engineered to shift emotional tone without collapsing the structure. The perfume works with every combination of the base and any/all modules.

Layering instead relies on mixing multiple perfumes together as people wear one perfume to work and another at night. This risks muddled scents and multiple notes. Using a specific base which provides the longest-lasting fragrance and adding modules that are primarily the middle and top notes allows a true shifting in the flavor of the fragrance.

For launch, the men’s base centers bergamot, lavender, cedarwood, and ambroxan, while the women’s base centers bergamot, orange blossom, vanilla, and white musk. Expression capsules such as iris, sandalwood, and amber or amberwood introduce small directional shifts rather than wholesale reformulation. This follows the project’s core rule: the base stays intact and the change stays subtle enough that the signature never gets muddled. The notes are also familiar in both fragrances with only part of the base changing for women's vs men's.

Men’s Base

Bergamot, lavender, cedarwood, ambroxan, then subtle movement through iris, sandalwood, or amberwood.

Women’s Base

Bergamot, orange blossom, vanilla, white musk, then subtle movement through iris, sandalwood, or amber.

Luxury Guardrail

The scent evolves in a controlled way rather than collapsing into a random blend.

That is commercially important. The dupe and layering culture around fragrance is growing, but uncontrolled layering frequently produces weak top notes and muddled drydowns. Scent in Motion turns that consumer behavior into a disciplined, premium alternative.

02

Mixing During Spray: The Core Mechanical Innovation

At the heart of the concept is a precision multi-channel microfluidic dispensing system that blends fragrance components only at the moment of spray. The bottle contains separate sealed reservoirs for the couture base and the expression modules. Each chamber connects to a micro-metering pump with calibrated flow valves.

When the actuator is pressed, pressure is distributed across the channels and releases precisely measured micro-volumes that converge in a micro-mixing chamber positioned directly before the atomizing nozzle. Using coaxial atomization geometry, the streams are rapidly sheared into a fine aerosol plume that produces a homogeneous scent cloud while preserving the stability of each formula during storage.

Because the liquids remain isolated until the final milliseconds of spray, the perfumer’s base architecture stays chemically intact while still allowing controlled modulation.

Dual Reservoir Architecture

Separate sealed chambers store the couture base and expression modules independently. This preserves formula stability during storage and prevents premature chemical interaction between ingredients.

Micro-Metering Pump System

Calibrated dual-piston micro-metering pumps release precise micro-volumes from each reservoir when the actuator is pressed, ensuring repeatable base-to-module ratios in every spray.

Micro-Mixing Atomization

The fragrance streams converge in a micro-mixing chamber directly before the atomizing nozzle. Coaxial atomization rapidly shears the liquids into a uniform aerosol plume, creating a consistent hybrid scent cloud.

From a manufacturing perspective, the launch configuration uses a dual-chamber architecture with one couture base reservoir and one expression module. This design relies on a dual-piston metering pump and an internal separator cartridge. At production scale, the mechanical system, luxury bottle components, and fragrance liquids are estimated to cost approximately $18–$32 per unit.

The platform can also expand to a four-chamber configuration with one base reservoir and up to three expression modules. This version requires a more complex multi-channel pump and mixing manifold, increasing manufacturing cost by approximately $20 per unit. These numbers are based on approximation and current industry costs rather than specific supplier quotes.

By embedding precision fluid mechanics into the bottle itself, the system turns fragrance modulation into a repeatable dispensing technology platform that is significantly more difficult to replicate than traditional refill or layering concepts.

03

A Bespoke Version for High-Value Clients

There is also a premium extension path. For select customers, Scent in Motion could be offered as a higher-cost bespoke edition where the wearer’s olfactive profile is first mapped through a Scent-Sation-style calibration session, then translated into a private base-plus-module system built around their own preferred scent movement.

YSL Beauty already tried Scent-Sation as a 25-minute in-store experience that uses neuroscience and AI to analyze emotional reactions to scent pairings and recommend tailored fragrances. Scent in Motion builds naturally on that logic: instead of only recommending an existing SKU, the service would configure a custom shifting scent profile with controlled ratios and a private emotional signature.

Standard model: curated bases and modules, scaled for broader retail and e-commerce launch.

Private-client model: a custom base-plus-module system informed by personal olfactive calibration.

Estimated premium tier: a standard system around $185, with a calibrated private-client edition in the $350 to $450 range.

04

AI as an Olfactive Curator

The AI Mood Translator functions as a guidance system rather than a creative engine. Its role is to interpret mood, context, and desired scent intensity, then translate those signals into a recommendation that remains within the perfumer’s original composition. Instead of generating new fragrances, it works inside a predefined olfactive architecture, preserving the integrity of haute perfumery while allowing subtle adaptation throughout the day.

Controlled Intelligence

The system evaluates mood, time of day, social setting, and projection level, then recommends a calibrated capsule adjustment that enhances the base without disrupting it.

Environmental Awareness

Real-time weather inputs such as temperature and humidity can refine recommendations, allowing lighter projection in heat or richer expression in cooler conditions, always within perfumer guidelines.

Over time, the AI learns from recurring behaviors and builds a Signature Profile shaped by capsule selection, usage patterns, and context. Rather than replacing the ritual of choosing fragrance, it refines it, making each recommendation feel increasingly personal.

This creates a stronger luxury story. The technology is not positioned as an engine that invents perfume formulas. It deepens the relationship between the wearer and the perfumer’s work by translating emotion, environment, and personal preference into subtle adjustments inside a curated creative framework.
