Inclusivity

Inclusivity

This page focuses on physical and cognitive ease: better grip, a lower-force spray gesture, safer travel closure, and easier scent discovery.

01

Ergonomic Bottle Form

The bottle should feel hand-contoured without becoming a one-off custom form. The aim is a sculpted silhouette that supports a wider range of grips and handling styles, making the object steadier, easier to rotate, and more intuitive to use. That benefits smaller hands, lower grip strength, and one-handed use without making the product look clinical.

In other words, ergonomics becomes an invisible luxury upgrade rather than an added accommodation.

02

Lower-Force Spray Actuator

Traditional perfume atomizers assume easy finger strength and repetitive pressing. Scent in Motion can improve that through a lower-force spray actuator that reduces the effort needed to dispense fragrance. That is highly relevant for users with arthritis, wrist strain, joint pain, or carpal tunnel symptoms, but it is also simply a better everyday experience.

Functional benefit: easier spray action means less strain and more consistent use.

Strategic support: L’Oréal’s own accessibility work, including HAPTA, already demonstrates a strong internal precedent for improving fine-motor beauty rituals through engineering.

Luxury benefit: a better actuator feels smoother and more premium for every user, not only for those with mobility challenges.

03

Magnetic Travel Closure

A magnetic close clasp or self-seating cap prevents accidental sprays in transit while reducing the dexterity needed to close the bottle accurately. That helps in bags, on flights, during commutes, and in one-handed use cases. It is an accessibility solution that also functions as a luxury gesture.

The closure becomes both a practical safeguard and a premium moment of control.

04

Guided Discovery for More Users

Inclusivity is also cognitive. Fragrance can be intimidating because it often depends on note literacy, in-store confidence, or trial-and-error. The Mood Translator reduces that barrier by helping users navigate scent choices through mood and context rather than specialist vocabulary.

That broadens the audience without diluting the brand. It makes the category easier to enter while keeping the experience refined.
